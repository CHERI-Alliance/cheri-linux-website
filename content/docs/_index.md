The focus of the Linux Strategy Working Group at CHERI Alliance is cross-architecture development work to adapt the Linux Kernel and userspace software to benefit from CHERI’s memory protection and software compartmentalization features.

## Join the Working Group

We hold open video calls every two weeks to coordinate porting Linux to CHERI. Whether you're an experienced Linux Kernel or userspace developer or just getting started with CHERI, your contributions are welcome. Membership in CHERI Alliance is not required to participate. The topics we've covered in previous calls are captured in the [meeting notes](/docs/meetings/notes/).

For more information, join the [mailing list](https://lists.cheri-alliance.org/mailman3/postorius/lists/linux-kernel.cheri-alliance.org/), chat with us on the [cheri-linux channel in the public CHERI-CPU Slack](https://cheri-cpu.slack.com/messages/cheri-linux), or email the [working group lead](mailto:allison.randal@cheri-alliance.org).

## What we work on

* Defining and prioritising the roadmap for porting Linux to CHERI
* Porting and testing across Morello, CHERI-RISC-V, QEMU, and FPGA platforms
* Toolchain integration — LLVM, QEMU, Yocto, and debugger support
* Engaging upstream Linux and open source communities to adopt CHERI changes
* Reviewing pull requests and tracking open issues on shared repositories
